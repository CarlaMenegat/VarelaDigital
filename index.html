The Project

Varela Digital is a digital edition and knowledge website dedicated to the letters of the Varela Collection, a group of documents written and received by Domingos José de Almeida and other figures involved in the Farroupilha Revolution (1835–1845) and organized by the historian Alfredo Varela. The pilot corpus is based on the letters published in volume II of the Anais do Arquivo Histórico do Rio Grande do Sul, encoded in TEI–XML and enriched with semantic annotation layers.

The edition offers a faceted navigation system enabling users to explore the corpus through key analytical categories: sender, addressee, place, date, and theme. Each transcription is accompanied by rich contextual metadata and editorial notes, deepening understanding of the political, geographic, and interpersonal networks represented in the documents.

Beyond textual representation, Varela Digital develops a structured knowledge base involving correspondents, institutions, events, places, and manuscript traditions. These datasets are made available as interoperable RDF/LOD resources that facilitate reuse, citation, and integration with external knowledge platforms.

The project also includes interactive visualizations such as maps and network graphs that reveal spatial dynamics and social patterns in the circulation of letters. Full documentation of the editorial workflow ensures academic transparency and reproducibility.
