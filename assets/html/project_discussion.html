Project Discussion

This page presents the conceptual rationale and editorial approach of Varela Digital. It explains how the project moves from documentary sources to structured representations—TEI, metadata, and Linked Data—in order to support historically meaningful navigation, analysis, and visualisation.

Scope and rationale

Varela Digital as a scholarly edition and knowledge environment

Varela Digital is conceived as a scholarly digital edition that goes beyond the online presentation of historical texts. It combines established practices in digital scholarly editing with data modelling and semantic representation, transforming the edition into a knowledge-oriented research environment.

Documents are not treated as isolated textual artefacts, but as historical objects situated within networks of people, places, institutions, events, and actions. The project adopts principles of transparency, explicit modelling choices, and reproducibility, extending them through TEI/XML encoding and Linked Open Data–oriented structures.

Knowledge base

In Varela Digital, the notion of “knowledge base” refers to the project’s capacity to make documentary evidence computable without detaching it from its editorial and archival grounding. The edition therefore operates as a layered knowledge environment: documents remain the primary scholarly objects, while persons, places, institutions, and events are modelled as explicit entities connected by traceable relations.

This knowledge base is not conceived as a generic repository of decontextualised facts. Instead, it is a curated representation built from editorial decisions (TEI/XML), operational metadata (CSV), authority files, and a semantic layer (RDF/JSON-LD). Each layer supports a different mode of access: close reading in the viewer, document-level navigation through indexes, and cross-document exploration through spatial and network visualisations.

Visualisations as interfaces to the knowledge base

The map and network views are not ancillary outputs, but user-facing interfaces to the underlying structured data. The correspondence map foregrounds spatial circulation and administrative geography, while social and institutional networks highlight patterns of interaction and responsibility among historical actors.

Together with metadata-driven indexes, these views enable users to move from individual documents to corpus-level questions, and back to the textual evidence that sustains each relation.

Access, reuse, and publication

Rather than exposing a public SPARQL endpoint in this pilot phase, the project prioritises lightweight and reproducible publication. The semantic layer is released as RDF/JSON-LD and used to feed the site’s navigational components and visualisation pipeline. This approach preserves interoperability while keeping the platform compatible with static web hosting.

Editorial and interpretive stance

Varela Digital adopts an editorial stance grounded in transparency and controlled interpretation. The edition preserves the editorial logic of the printed Anais do Arquivo Histórico do Rio Grande do Sul, explicitly documenting its scope, limits, and methodological assumptions.

From texts to structured data

The project is built as a layered environment in which TEI/XML serves as the authoritative textual representation, metadata supports navigation, and RDF/JSON-LD enables semantic modelling and visualisation.

Reuse and interoperability

Interoperability is ensured through the reuse of shared standards and vocabularies, combined with the minimal domain-specific extension provided by HRAO.

Current state and roadmap

Varela Digital is currently implemented as a functional pilot, supporting close reading, metadata-driven browsing, spatial visualisation of correspondence, and multiple network views derived from explicitly modelled relations.

Future developments will focus on extending the corpus, refining the knowledge base, and strengthening alignment with external datasets and research infrastructures.
