Documentation

This page describes how Varela Digital was built: editorial workflow, encoding practices, and data modelling choices. It complements the Historical Context page and supports the reuse and evaluation of the edition.

Open the Viewer Explore Indexes Data Model Theoretical Framework

What this documentation covers

  • Corpus and scope: the pilot selection (CV-1 to CV-300) and its rationale.
  • Editorial workflow: how TEI files are produced, revised, and published.
  • Encoding strategy: what is encoded in the documents vs. in the standoff files.
  • Data modelling: how documents, people, places, and relations are represented.
  • Outputs: TEI/XML, JSON-LD, and RDF/Turtle, plus planned visualizations.

Workflow at a glance

  1. Source text (printed transcriptions in the Anais) is curated into a pilot corpus (CV-1 to CV-300).
  2. TEI/XML encoding captures document structure and editorial features (dates, places, people, notes).
  3. Standoff enrichment aggregates authority information (persons, places, institutions) and cross-document links.
  4. Transformation pipeline generates HTML for reading and exports structured data (JSON-LD / RDF).
  5. Exploration layer provides viewer navigation, indexes, and (future) maps/networks.
Design principle: the edition is both a reading interface and a structured research dataset. Modelling decisions follow historical questions (actors, institutions, circulation, and events) rather than purely technical convenience.

Data and reuse

The project publishes TEI/XML as the primary editorial format and provides machine-actionable exports (JSON-LD and RDF/Turtle) to support reuse, querying, and future visualizations. Source references remain anchored in the documentary units and editorial structure of the Anais.

Planned extensions
  • Expanded indexes (mentioned entities, roles, and relational browsing).
  • Geographic visualization of correspondence circulation.
  • Network visualization of people, institutions, and events across letters.
  • Integration of manuscript images and a diplomatic transcription layer (future development).
